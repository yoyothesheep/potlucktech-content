# Potluck Technologies — Tone Guide

## Author Assignment

- **Sha-Mayn Teh** — CTO, engineering, technical leadership topics
- **Yu Chen** — Product, coaching, career, AI strategy, opportunity-gap topics
- **Co-authored** — posts spanning both; lead author determined by primary topic

## Voice

Direct, practical, earned authority. Write like a senior operator talking to a peer founder — not a consultant, not a thought leader. No hedging, no jargon, no filler.

Use first-person "I" for personal experience. Use "we" for Potluck as a firm.

## Credibility Signals

Surface Google/TpT/Flatiron/client experience when it's genuinely relevant to the point being made. Never name-drop for its own sake.

## Tone Markers — Hit These

- Lead with the answer, then explain
- Specific over vague ("15 engineers" not "a growing team")
- Honest about tradeoffs and when NOT to hire Potluck
- Short sentences under load; longer sentences for nuance
- Sailing/adaptability metaphors — OK for Sha-Mayn
- Opportunity-gap and education-as-equalizer framing — OK for Yu Chen

## Tone Markers — Avoid These

**Consultant-speak:** "leverage," "synergies," "move the needle," "at the end of the day," "robust," "scalable solution"

**Hedging without follow-through:** "it depends," "there's no one-size-fits-all," "every situation is unique" — only acceptable if immediately followed by a specific framework or decision rule

**Inflated credentials:** "world-class," "industry-leading," "cutting-edge," "top-tier"

**Generic CTAs:** "reach out to learn more," "let's chat," "feel free to contact us"

## Potluck Positioning to Reinforce

- Embed, don't advise from a distance
- Two female ex-Googlers — relevant context, not a marketing badge
- Early-stage specialist — 0 to Series A is the sweet spot; be honest when something is out of scope
- The goal of a fractional engagement is to make itself unnecessary — say this directly
- Reference Potluck-specific examples and domains when possible (e.g., tech for social impact, edtech, or the combined all-in CTO/CPO/devs team package)

## Self-Evaluation Checklist

When reviewing a draft against this guide, flag any instance of:

1. A section that doesn't lead with the answer
2. A vague claim that could be made specific with a number or named example
3. Any word or phrase from the "avoid" list above
4. A CTA that sounds generic rather than self-selecting
5. Author voice mismatch (e.g. CTO topic written in Yu Chen's voice or vice versa)
6. Credentials surfaced without a reason relevant to the point being made

## Target Personas

### Persona 1: The Non-Technical Founder
**Role:** CEO/Founder, no engineering background
**Stage:** Pre-seed to Seed ($0–$3M raised)
**Core anxiety:** "I don't know what I don't know. I'm spending money on engineers and I can't tell if we're building the right thing, at the right speed, with the right people."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "What is a fractional CTO and how does it work?", "How much does a fractional CTO cost?", "When should I hire a fractional CTO?"
**Reddit language they use:** "Do I need a CTO or just a senior dev?", "Can my first engineering hire be a CTO?"
**What Potluck offers them:** Fractional CTO engagement — strategy, vendor/contractor oversight, architecture decisions, technical hiring guidance.

### Persona 2: The Series A Founder Ready to Scale
**Role:** CEO/Co-founder (may be technical or non-technical)
**Stage:** Series A ($3M–$15M raised), 10–40 person company
**Core anxiety:** "We have product-market fit but our tech is a mess. We're hiring fast but losing culture. My engineers are burning out. I need someone who's done this before."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "Fractional CTO vs full-time CTO — which is right for my stage?", "Scaling engineering teams without losing culture"
**Reddit language they use:** "How do I scale from 1 engineer to 10+ without losing culture?", "Promoting first engineer to CTO creates title/role problems that are hard to undo"
**What Potluck offers them:** Fractional CTO or fractional CPO — scaling strategy, team architecture, technical debt triage, and engineering culture.

### Persona 3: The First-Time Engineering Manager
**Role:** Senior IC promoted to Engineering Manager (EM), 3–8 years experience
**Stage:** Recently promoted or considering promotion
**Core anxiety:** "I was good at writing code. Now I'm supposed to manage people and I have no idea what I'm doing. I miss the feedback loops. I don't know if I'm failing."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "What is engineering leadership coaching and do I need it?"
**Reddit language they use:** "I just got promoted to EM — what should I focus on first?", "How do I stop micromanaging as a new EM?", "The transition feels lonely"
**What Potluck offers them:** 1:1 leadership coaching, IC-to-EM transition support.

### Persona 4: The Aspiring Staff Engineer
**Role:** Senior Software Engineer (L5/L6), 6–12 years experience
**Stage:** Gunning for Staff or Principal
**Core anxiety:** "I don't code as much as I used to, but I'm not sure I'm actually leading anything. My impact is invisible."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "What does a staff engineer actually spend their time on?", "How do I measure my own impact as a senior IC?"
**Reddit language they use:** "Influence without authority", "Slow feedback loops — demoralizing and isolating"
**What Potluck offers them:** Leadership coaching for individual contributors through executive level.

### Persona 5: The AI-Curious Founder
**Role:** CEO or CTO at a startup trying to integrate AI
**Stage:** Seed to Series B, product exists
**Core anxiety:** "Everyone says we need to be an AI company. I don't know what that means for our product. I don't want to waste six months building something our users don't need."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "Fractional CTO for AI strategy — how do I implement AI?", "What AI use cases make sense for my startup?"
**What Potluck offers them:** AI stack building from a Google veteran team. Strongest competitive differentiator.

### Persona 6: The Social Impact Tech Founder
**Role:** Founder/CEO of a mission-driven tech startup
**Stage:** Seed to Series A
**Core anxiety:** "I'm caught between the pressure from investors to 'move fast and break things' and our ethical obligation to build accessible, unbiased tech for vulnerable populations."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "How to balance social impact with technical scalability?", "Finding a CTO who cares about mission, not just equity"
**Reddit language they use:** "How do I find a technical partner who won't just treat us like a stepping stone?", "Tension between shipping fast and building ethically"
**What Potluck offers them:** A Fractional CTO who understands how to build lean, secure, and accessible systems for underserved populations without compromising the mission.

### Persona 7: The Non-Profit Tech Founder
**Role:** Founder of a tech platform structured as a 501(c)(3)
**Stage:** Operating on restricted grants, building an MVP or scaling a platform
**Core anxiety:** "I'm trying to build a software platform using grant money, not VC funding. I can't offer equity to a technical co-founder, and dev shops are draining my budget."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "How to build tech for a non-profit without a technical cofounder?", "Fractional CTO for 501c3 tech platform"
**Reddit language they use:** "Can't offer equity because we're a non-profit—how do I attract a CTO?", "Grants only cover programmatic work, not core infrastructure."
**What Potluck offers them:** Strategic technical leadership that aligns with grant-based funding constraints. Help vetting contractors or building an internal team without needing to offer startup equity.

### Persona 8: The Non-Profit Tech Leader
**Role:** Executive Director / Director of IT at a traditional non-profit
**Stage:** Established non-profit undergoing digital transformation
**Core anxiety:** "We have a beer budget for champagne tastes. Our legacy systems are a mess, we handle sensitive donor data, and I can't afford a $250K+ full-time CTO. I don't need IT support; I need tech strategy."
**Questions they're asking AI engines:** "Fractional CTO for charities", "Tech audit and digital transformation for non-profits"
**Reddit language they use:** "We're stuck with technical debt and zero budget to fix it.", "Person-role conflation: I'm the ED but also the de facto IT person."
**What Potluck offers them:** Enterprise-grade strategic guidance at a fraction of the cost to untangle legacy systems, define technical roles, and implement secure data strategies.
