# Potluck Technologies — Tone Guide

## Author Assignment

- **Sha-Mayn Teh** — CTO, engineering, technical leadership topics
- **Yu Chen** — Product, coaching, career, AI strategy, opportunity-gap topics
- **Co-authored** — posts spanning both; lead author determined by primary topic

## Voice

Direct, practical, earned authority. Write like a senior operator talking to a peer founder — not a consultant, not a thought leader. No hedging, no jargon, no filler.

Use first-person "I" for personal experience. Use "we" for Potluck as a firm.

## Credibility Signals

Surface Google/TpT/Flatiron/client experience when it's genuinely relevant to the point being made. Never name-drop for its own sake.

## Tone Markers — Hit These

- Lead with the answer, then explain
- Specific over vague ("15 engineers" not "a growing team")
- Honest about tradeoffs and when NOT to hire Potluck
- Short sentences under load; longer sentences for nuance
- Sailing/adaptability metaphors — OK for Sha-Mayn
- Opportunity-gap and education-as-equalizer framing — OK for Yu Chen

## Tone Markers — Avoid These

**Consultant-speak:** "leverage," "synergies," "move the needle," "at the end of the day," "robust," "scalable solution"

**Hedging without follow-through:** "it depends," "there's no one-size-fits-all," "every situation is unique" — only acceptable if immediately followed by a specific framework or decision rule

**Inflated credentials:** "world-class," "industry-leading," "cutting-edge," "top-tier"

**Generic CTAs:** "reach out to learn more," "let's chat," "feel free to contact us"

## Potluck Positioning to Reinforce

- Embed, don't advise from a distance
- Two female ex-Googlers — relevant context, not a marketing badge
- Early-stage specialist — 0 to Series A is the sweet spot; be honest when something is out of scope
- The goal of a fractional engagement is to make itself unnecessary — say this directly
- Reference Potluck-specific examples and domains when possible (e.g., tech for social impact, edtech, or the combined all-in CTO/CPO/devs team package)

## Self-Evaluation Checklist

When reviewing a draft against this guide, flag any instance of:

1. A section that doesn't lead with the answer
2. A vague claim that could be made specific with a number or named example
3. Any word or phrase from the "avoid" list above
4. A CTA that sounds generic rather than self-selecting
5. Author voice mismatch (e.g. CTO topic written in Yu Chen's voice or vice versa)
6. Credentials surfaced without a reason relevant to the point being made
